Q4 2024: Record Revenue as Platform Adoption Accelerates Across Enterprise Segment

Revenue grew 34% year-over-year, driven by expansion in financial services and healthcare verticals. Management raises full-year guidance amid strong enterprise demand.

SM
Sarah Mitchell Chief Financial Correspondent
December 26, 2024 8 min read

DataBao reported record quarterly revenue of $287 million for Q4 2024, representing a 34% increase year-over-year and beating analyst expectations by 12%. The results cap off a transformative year for the company, which has emerged as a leading player in the enterprise data analytics market.

"This quarter demonstrates the power of our platform strategy," said CEO Michael Torres during the earnings call. "We're seeing unprecedented adoption across our target verticals, and our expansion into Asia-Pacific is already exceeding our initial projections."

Key Financial Highlights

The quarter was marked by several notable achievements:

  • Revenue: $287 million, up 34% YoY
  • Annual Recurring Revenue (ARR): $1.1 billion, crossing the billion-dollar threshold
  • Net Revenue Retention: 128%, up from 118% in Q4 2023
  • Gross Margin: 78%, improved by 200 basis points
  • Free Cash Flow: $42 million, marking the third consecutive quarter of positive FCF
"We're not just growing; we're growing profitably. This is a fundamentally different company than we were 18 months ago." — David Kim, Chief Financial Officer

Enterprise Segment Drives Growth

The enterprise segment, which includes customers with annual contracts exceeding $100,000, accounted for 72% of total revenue, up from 64% in the prior year. The company added 47 new enterprise customers during the quarter, bringing the total to 312.

"We're seeing deals close faster and expand faster than ever before."

Jennifer Walsh, Chief Revenue Officer

Particularly notable was the performance in the financial services vertical, which grew 52% year-over-year. The company attributed this growth to its enhanced compliance and security features, which have become table stakes for regulated industries.

Geographic Expansion

International revenue now represents 38% of total revenue, up from 29% a year ago. The recently opened Singapore hub is expected to accelerate this trend, with management projecting international revenue to reach 45% of total revenue by the end of 2025.

The company also announced plans to open additional offices in London and Frankfurt in the first half of 2025, reflecting strong demand from European enterprise customers.

Product Updates

During the quarter, DataBao launched Platform 3.0, which includes AI-powered analytics and real-time collaboration features. Early adoption metrics have been encouraging, with 67% of existing customers already migrated to the new platform.

"Platform 3.0 represents a fundamental shift in how enterprises interact with their data," said CTO Priya Sharma. "The AI capabilities we've built aren't just features—they're force multipliers for our customers' data teams."

Outlook and Guidance

For Q1 2025, the company expects revenue between $295 million and $302 million, representing 28-31% year-over-year growth. Full-year 2025 revenue is expected to be between $1.25 billion and $1.30 billion.

The company also announced a $500 million stock buyback program, signaling confidence in its long-term trajectory and commitment to returning value to shareholders.

Analyst Reactions

Wall Street responded positively to the results, with the stock rising 8% in after-hours trading. Several analysts raised their price targets:

  • Morgan Stanley: Raised to $185 from $160, maintaining Overweight rating
  • Goldman Sachs: Raised to $190 from $155, upgraded to Buy
  • JP Morgan: Raised to $180 from $165, maintaining Overweight rating

"DataBao continues to execute flawlessly in a challenging macro environment," wrote Morgan Stanley analyst Keith Weiss. "The combination of revenue growth and improving profitability is rare in enterprise software today."
